Claire Dux (2 August 1885, Witkowicz - 8 October 1967, Chicago) was a Polish operatic soprano. After studying singing in Berlin, she made made her professional opera debut in 1906 at the Cologne Opera as Pamina in Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's The Magic Flute. From 1911-1918 she was a member of the Berlin State Opera. She made her London debut in 1911 singing with Thomas Beecham at His Majesty's Theatre. Two years later she sang for the first time at the Royal Opera House in that city as Sophie in Richard Strauss' Der Rosenkavalier. In 1921 she became a member of the Chicago Civic Opera where she remained for the rest of her career; although she returned to Europe occassionally as a guest artist. She lived her retirement years in Chicago.
